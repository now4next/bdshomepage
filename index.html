Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about BDS admissions, academics, and campus life

BDS accepts students in grades 9-12 who demonstrate academic potential, curiosity, and a commitment to personal growth. The application process includes:

  • Completed application form with personal essay
  • Academic transcripts from previous schools
  • English proficiency test (TOEFL/IELTS for international students)
  • Two teacher recommendations
  • Interview with admissions committee

We evaluate each applicant holistically, considering not just grades but also character, motivation, and alignment with our educational philosophy.

BDS is one of the few schools worldwide that integrates daily meditation into its curriculum. Every morning begins with a 20-30 minute guided meditation session designed to:

  • Develop focus and mental clarity for academic work
  • Build emotional resilience and stress management skills
  • Cultivate mindfulness and self-awareness
  • Foster inner peace and balanced decision-making

Students consistently report that meditation helps them manage college application stress, improve concentration, and maintain emotional balance—skills they carry into university and beyond.

"Dream Beyond a Dream" is BDS's core educational philosophy that challenges students to think beyond personal success. Instead of dreaming only for themselves, we encourage students to ask:

  • "How can my dreams benefit others?"
  • "What positive impact can I make on my community and the world?"
  • "How can I use my talents to address global challenges?"

This philosophy shapes everything from our curriculum to our community service projects, creating graduates who are not just academically successful but also socially responsible global citizens.

BDS maintains a 100% university acceptance rate through comprehensive college preparation:

  • Rigorous Academics: AP courses, SAT/TOEFL preparation, and college-level writing
  • Small Classes: 8:1 student-teacher ratio ensures personalized guidance
  • College Counseling: Dedicated counselors assist with applications, essays, and interviews
  • Extracurriculars: Leadership opportunities, community service, and unique programs
  • Global Perspective: International student body prepares students for diverse campus environments

Our graduates have been accepted to Stanford, UC Berkeley, Yale, MIT, and leading universities in Korea, the UK, and beyond.

BDS campus life balances rigorous academics with holistic personal development:

  • Natural Setting: Located in Chungju's serene countryside, providing a peaceful learning environment
  • Residential Community: Dormitory living fosters independence and close friendships
  • International Environment: Students from 15+ countries create a truly global community
  • Daily Schedule: Morning meditation, academic classes, sports/arts, evening study, and community time
  • Healthy Living: Organic temple-style meals, outdoor activities, and wellness programs

Students describe BDS as a supportive family where they grow academically, emotionally, and spiritually.

BDS offers comprehensive tuition packages and financial assistance:

  • Tuition: Covers academics, boarding, meals, and most activities
  • Merit Scholarships: Available for students with outstanding academic or leadership records
  • Need-Based Aid: Financial assistance for families demonstrating need
  • Payment Plans: Flexible payment options available

We believe exceptional education should be accessible. Please contact our admissions office at admin@bds-korea.org or +82-70-7525-5517 for detailed tuition information and to discuss financial aid options.
